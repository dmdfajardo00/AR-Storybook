## Question 1

What do plants absorb from the air during photosynthesis?

- [ ] Oxygen
  > Nice try! Oxygen is actually what plants release during photosynthesis, not what they absorb. Think about what gas humans breathe out that plants need!

- [x] Carbon dioxide
  > Excellent! Plants absorb carbon dioxide (CO2) from the air and use sunlight to convert it into food through photosynthesis. You're a plant expert!

- [ ] Nitrogen
  > Nice try! While nitrogen is important for plants, they get it from the soil through their roots, not from the air during photosynthesis.

- [ ] Helium
  > Nice try! Helium is a gas that makes balloons float, but plants don't use it at all. Plants need carbon dioxide for photosynthesis!

> Hint: Think about what plants need to make their food - it's a gas that humans and animals breathe out!

## Question 6

What process do plants use to absorb carbon dioxide?

- [ ] Respiration
  > Nice try! Respiration is actually how living things use oxygen and release CO2. Plants absorb CO2 through a different process that uses sunlight!

- [ ] Evaporation
  > Nice try! Evaporation is when water turns into vapor. Plants absorb CO2 through a special process that involves sunlight and making food!

- [x] Photosynthesis
  > Brilliant! Photosynthesis is the amazing process where plants use sunlight, water, and carbon dioxide to make their own food. Photo means light, synthesis means putting together!

- [ ] Hibernation
  > Nice try! Hibernation is when animals sleep for a long time in winter. Plants absorb carbon dioxide through photosynthesis instead!

> Hint: This process name comes from Greek words meaning 'light' and 'putting together'!

## Question 7

What keeps the carbon cycle balanced and healthy?

- [ ] Only having plants on Earth
  > Nice try! We need both plants AND animals for a balanced carbon cycle. Plants absorb CO2, and animals help release it back - it's teamwork!

- [x] The natural exchange between plants, animals, and the atmosphere
  > Exactly right! A healthy carbon cycle needs plants absorbing CO2, animals releasing CO2, and everything working together in balance. Nature is amazing at teamwork!

- [ ] Burning lots of fossil fuels
  > Nice try! Burning fossil fuels actually puts too much carbon into the air and throws the cycle out of balance. The natural exchange is what keeps it healthy!

- [ ] Removing all carbon from the Earth
  > Nice try! We actually need carbon - it's essential for all life! The key is keeping it balanced, not removing it completely.

> Hint: Think about how plants and animals work together like a team!

## Question 8

When a tree grows bigger, what is it doing with carbon?

- [ ] Releasing it into the air
  > Nice try! When trees grow, they're actually taking carbon OUT of the air and storing it in their wood. That's why planting trees helps our planet!

- [x] Storing it in its wood and leaves
  > Wonderful! As trees grow bigger, they store more and more carbon in their wood, bark, leaves, and roots. A big tree is like a giant carbon storage tank! Great thinking!

- [ ] Sending it to other planets
  > Nice try! Trees can't send carbon to other planets. Instead, they store carbon right here on Earth in their wood, leaves, and roots as they grow!

- [ ] Destroying the carbon completely
  > Nice try! Carbon can't be destroyed - it just moves around! Trees store carbon in their wood and leaves, keeping it safe from the atmosphere.

> Hint: Think about what the trunk and branches of a tree are made of!
