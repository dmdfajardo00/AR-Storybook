## Question 1

What do greenhouse gases do?

- [ ] They cool down the Earth
  > Nice try! Greenhouse gases actually do the opposite - they trap heat and warm up our planet, like a cozy blanket.

- [x] They trap heat in the atmosphere
  > Excellent! Greenhouse gases act like a blanket, trapping heat from the sun and keeping Earth warm enough for life.

- [ ] They create rain clouds
  > Nice try! While water vapor is a greenhouse gas, the main job of greenhouse gases is to trap heat, not create rain.

- [ ] They block all sunlight from reaching Earth
  > Nice try! Greenhouse gases actually let sunlight through to warm the Earth, then trap some of that heat so it doesn't escape back into space.

> Hint: Think about what a blanket does when you're cold!

## Question 2

Why is the greenhouse effect compared to a blanket?

- [x] Because it makes Earth comfortable and warm
  > You got it! Just like a blanket keeps you warm by trapping your body heat, greenhouse gases trap heat from the sun to keep Earth at a comfortable temperature.

- [ ] Because it's soft and fluffy
  > Nice try! The comparison is about how it works, not how it feels. Both blankets and greenhouse gases trap heat!

- [ ] Because you can see it from space
  > Nice try! You can't actually see the greenhouse effect - it's an invisible process. The blanket comparison is about trapping warmth!

- [ ] Because it covers the entire Earth like fabric
  > Nice try! While greenhouse gases do surround Earth, the blanket comparison is really about how they trap heat, just like your blanket keeps you warm at night.

## Question 3

Which of these is a greenhouse gas?

- [ ] Oxygen
  > Nice try! Oxygen is the gas we breathe to stay alive, but it doesn't trap heat. Carbon dioxide and methane are examples of greenhouse gases.

- [x] Carbon dioxide
  > Great job! Carbon dioxide (CO2) is one of the most important greenhouse gases. It comes from burning fossil fuels and breathing!

- [ ] Nitrogen
  > Nice try! Nitrogen makes up most of our atmosphere (78%), but it doesn't trap heat like greenhouse gases do.

- [ ] Helium
  > Nice try! Helium is a light gas that makes balloons float, but it's not a greenhouse gas. Carbon dioxide is a good example of one!

> Hint: This gas has 'carbon' in its name and plants use it for photosynthesis.

## Question 4

Is the greenhouse effect always bad for Earth?

- [ ] Yes, it only causes problems
  > Nice try! Without some greenhouse effect, Earth would be too cold for life! It's only a problem when there are TOO MANY greenhouse gases.

- [x] No, we need some greenhouse effect to keep Earth warm enough for life
  > Exactly right! Without the natural greenhouse effect, Earth would be about 60 degrees Fahrenheit colder - too cold for most life! We just don't want too much of it.

- [ ] The greenhouse effect doesn't affect Earth at all
  > Nice try! The greenhouse effect has a huge impact on Earth - it's what keeps our planet warm enough for plants, animals, and us to survive!

- [ ] Yes, we should try to remove all greenhouse gases
  > Nice try! If we removed all greenhouse gases, Earth would freeze! We need some greenhouse effect - we just need to stop adding too much extra.

## Question 5

What happens when we add too many greenhouse gases to the atmosphere?

- [ ] Earth cools down
  > Nice try! More greenhouse gases means more heat gets trapped, so Earth actually warms up, not cools down.

- [ ] Nothing changes
  > Nice try! Adding more greenhouse gases definitely changes things - it traps more heat and causes our planet to warm up!

- [x] Earth gets too warm
  > Correct! When we add too many greenhouse gases, it's like putting on too many blankets - Earth traps too much heat and gets warmer than it should be.

- [ ] The atmosphere disappears
  > Nice try! Greenhouse gases don't make the atmosphere disappear - they actually add to it and trap more heat, making Earth warmer.

> Hint: Think about what happens when you have too many blankets on your bed!

## Question 6

Where does most of the extra greenhouse gas in our atmosphere come from?

- [ ] Volcanoes
  > Nice try! Volcanoes do release some greenhouse gases, but human activities like burning fossil fuels add much more to our atmosphere.

- [x] Burning fossil fuels like coal, oil, and gas
  > You're a climate expert! Burning fossil fuels for cars, electricity, and factories is the biggest source of extra greenhouse gases in our atmosphere.

- [ ] Plants and trees
  > Nice try! Actually, plants help REMOVE greenhouse gases from the air! The extra gases mostly come from burning fossil fuels.

- [ ] The ocean
  > Nice try! The ocean actually absorbs greenhouse gases and helps reduce them. Most extra gases come from burning fossil fuels.

## Question 7

What is another name for the warming caused by too many greenhouse gases?

- [ ] Global cooling
  > Nice try! It's actually the opposite - too many greenhouse gases cause warming, not cooling. The correct term is global warming!

- [x] Global warming
  > Perfect! Global warming is the term scientists use to describe how extra greenhouse gases are making our planet's average temperature rise.

- [ ] Seasonal change
  > Nice try! Seasonal changes happen naturally throughout the year. The warming from greenhouse gases is called global warming.

- [ ] Weather forecast
  > Nice try! A weather forecast tells us what the weather will be like soon. The warming from greenhouse gases is called global warming.

## Question 8

How does heat from the sun interact with greenhouse gases?

- [ ] Greenhouse gases block all heat from entering Earth
  > Nice try! Greenhouse gases actually let heat in - they just trap some of it when it tries to escape back into space.

- [x] Heat passes through, but some gets trapped when trying to escape
  > Excellent thinking! Sunlight passes through greenhouse gases to warm Earth, but when that heat tries to leave, greenhouse gases trap some of it.

- [ ] Greenhouse gases push heat toward the sun
  > Nice try! Greenhouse gases don't push heat away - they actually trap heat near Earth's surface.

- [ ] Heat destroys greenhouse gases
  > Nice try! Heat doesn't destroy greenhouse gases. Instead, greenhouse gases trap the heat and keep it near Earth.

> Hint: Think about how light comes in through a greenhouse window but the warmth stays inside.

## Question 9

Besides carbon dioxide, which of these is also a greenhouse gas?

- [x] Methane
  > Great job! Methane is a powerful greenhouse gas that comes from cows, rice fields, and rotting garbage. It traps even more heat than carbon dioxide!

- [ ] Gold
  > Nice try! Gold is a metal, not a gas at all. Methane and water vapor are examples of other greenhouse gases.

- [ ] Sand
  > Nice try! Sand is a solid material, not a gas. Methane is another example of a greenhouse gas!

- [ ] Salt
  > Nice try! Salt is a mineral, not a gas. Greenhouse gases like methane and water vapor float in the atmosphere.

## Question 10

If Earth had no greenhouse effect at all, what would happen?

- [ ] Earth would be much hotter
  > Nice try! Without greenhouse gases to trap heat, Earth would actually be much colder because heat would escape into space!

- [ ] Earth would be about the same temperature
  > Nice try! The greenhouse effect makes a big difference - without it, Earth would be about 60 degrees Fahrenheit colder!

- [x] Earth would be frozen and too cold for most life
  > Exactly right! Without any greenhouse effect, Earth's average temperature would be around 0°F (-18°C) - way too cold for most plants and animals!

- [ ] Only the oceans would freeze
  > Nice try! Without greenhouse gases, almost everything would freeze, not just the oceans. We need some greenhouse effect to keep Earth livable!

> Hint: Remember, greenhouse gases work like a blanket to keep us warm!
