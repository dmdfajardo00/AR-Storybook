## Question 1

What happens when polar ice melts?

- [x] Sea levels rise
  > Exactly right! When ice on land melts, the water flows into the ocean and causes sea levels to rise, which can flood coastal areas.

- [ ] The ocean gets colder
  > Nice try! While melting ice is cold, the overall effect of climate change is warming oceans. The main concern is rising sea levels.

- [ ] More ice forms elsewhere
  > Nice try! Unfortunately, as Earth warms, we're losing more ice than we're gaining. The melted water adds to our oceans.

- [ ] The sky changes color
  > Nice try! Melting ice doesn't change the sky's color. The main effect is that sea levels rise when ice melts into the ocean.

> Hint: Think about where all that melted water goes!

## Question 2

Why is it important that ice reflects sunlight?

- [ ] It makes the poles look pretty
  > Nice try! While ice is beautiful, the important part is that when ice reflects sunlight, that energy goes back to space instead of warming Earth.

- [x] It helps keep Earth cool by sending heat back into space
  > Brilliant! White ice acts like a mirror, bouncing sunlight back into space. This helps keep our planet from getting too warm.

- [ ] It creates rainbows
  > Nice try! Ice can create some light effects, but its main job is reflecting sunlight away from Earth to help keep temperatures down.

- [ ] It doesn't matter if ice reflects light
  > Nice try! It matters a lot! When ice reflects sunlight, it keeps that energy from warming our planet. Less ice means more warming.

## Question 3

Which animals are most affected by melting Arctic ice?

- [ ] Lions and elephants
  > Nice try! Lions and elephants live in warm areas like Africa. Animals like polar bears need Arctic ice to hunt and survive.

- [x] Polar bears and seals
  > That's right! Polar bears need sea ice to hunt for seals, and seals use ice to rest and have their babies. Less ice means trouble for both!

- [ ] Butterflies and bees
  > Nice try! Butterflies and bees live in warmer places with flowers. Arctic animals like polar bears depend on ice for survival.

- [ ] Fish and sharks
  > Nice try! While ocean changes affect fish, the animals most directly impacted by melting ice are polar bears, seals, and walruses who live on the ice.

## Question 4

What is happening to ice at the North and South Poles?

- [ ] It's growing larger every year
  > Nice try! Actually, polar ice has been shrinking. Scientists have measured that we're losing billions of tons of ice each year.

- [ ] It stays exactly the same
  > Nice try! Polar ice is definitely changing - it's melting faster than ever before because of rising global temperatures.

- [x] It's melting faster than ever before
  > Correct! As Earth gets warmer, the ice at both poles is melting much faster than it used to. Some scientists worry it might disappear in summer!

- [ ] It's moving to different locations
  > Nice try! Ice doesn't move to new places - it melts where it is. The polar ice caps are getting smaller due to warming temperatures.

## Question 5

Which places could flood if sea levels rise too much?

- [ ] Mountain tops
  > Nice try! Mountains are high above the ocean, so they wouldn't flood. Coastal cities near the ocean are in danger of flooding.

- [x] Coastal cities and islands
  > You've got it! Cities near the ocean and low-lying islands could be flooded if sea levels rise too much from melting ice.

- [ ] Deserts
  > Nice try! Deserts are usually far from the ocean and won't flood from rising seas. Coastal areas are the ones at risk.

- [ ] The moon
  > Nice try! The moon doesn't have liquid water or oceans. Rising sea levels affect places near Earth's coasts.

> Hint: Think about which places are close to the ocean!

## Question 6

Where can you find penguins in the wild?

- [ ] The North Pole (Arctic)
  > Nice try! There are no wild penguins at the North Pole - that's where polar bears live! Penguins live near the South Pole.

- [x] The South Pole (Antarctica)
  > Perfect! Penguins live in the Southern Hemisphere, especially around Antarctica. They depend on cold ice and water to survive.

- [ ] The Sahara Desert
  > Nice try! Penguins need cold water and ice to survive. The hot Sahara Desert is definitely not their home!

- [ ] Tropical rainforests
  > Nice try! Penguins prefer cold climates with ice and cold water. They live near Antarctica in the Southern Hemisphere.

## Question 7

What color reflects the most sunlight?

- [ ] Black
  > Nice try! Black actually absorbs sunlight and heats up. That's why wearing black on a sunny day makes you feel hot!

- [ ] Blue
  > Nice try! Blue absorbs some sunlight. White is the best at reflecting light, which is why snow and ice help keep the poles cool.

- [x] White
  > Excellent! White surfaces like ice and snow reflect the most sunlight. This is why losing white ice is a problem - darker ocean water absorbs more heat!

- [ ] Green
  > Nice try! Green surfaces absorb some light. White ice and snow are the best at reflecting sunlight back into space.

> Hint: Think about what color ice and snow are!

## Question 8

What happens when white ice melts and reveals dark ocean water?

- [ ] The dark water reflects more light and cools Earth
  > Nice try! Actually, dark water absorbs more sunlight than white ice, which causes even more warming - not cooling.

- [x] The dark water absorbs more heat, causing even more warming
  > Exactly! This is called a 'feedback loop.' Dark water absorbs heat instead of reflecting it, which causes more ice to melt, revealing more dark water.

- [ ] Nothing changes
  > Nice try! This change matters a lot! Dark ocean water absorbs more heat than white ice, speeding up global warming.

- [ ] The water freezes faster
  > Nice try! Dark water absorbs more heat from the sun, making it warmer, not colder. This makes it harder for new ice to form.

## Question 9

About how much has sea level risen in the last 100 years?

- [ ] It hasn't risen at all
  > Nice try! Scientists have measured sea levels carefully, and they've definitely risen as ice has melted and water has expanded from warming.

- [x] About 8 inches (20 centimeters)
  > Great knowledge! Sea levels have risen about 8 inches in the last century, and scientists expect them to rise even faster in the future.

- [ ] About 100 feet (30 meters)
  > Nice try! That would be dramatic! Sea levels have risen about 8 inches, but scientists are concerned about future rise if we don't act.

- [ ] About 1 inch (2.5 centimeters)
  > Nice try! Sea levels have actually risen more than that - about 8 inches in the last 100 years, and the rate is speeding up.

> Hint: It's more than you might think, but not as dramatic as some options suggest!

## Question 10

Why do polar bears need sea ice?

- [ ] To stay cool in the summer
  > Nice try! Polar bears are built for cold weather. They need sea ice mainly because it's where they hunt for seals, their main food.

- [x] To hunt for seals, their main food source
  > Perfect! Polar bears are excellent swimmers, but they need ice platforms to rest on and catch seals. Less ice means hungry polar bears.

- [ ] To practice sliding
  > Nice try! While polar bears might enjoy ice, they really need it for hunting. Seals rest on ice, and that's where polar bears catch them.

- [ ] They don't actually need ice
  > Nice try! Polar bears definitely need ice! It's essential for hunting seals. Without enough ice, polar bears can't find enough food.
