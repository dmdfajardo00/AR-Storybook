## Question 1

Which action helps reduce carbon emissions?

- [ ] Leaving lights on all day
  > Nice try! Leaving lights on wastes electricity, which often comes from burning fossil fuels. Turning them off helps reduce emissions!

- [x] Walking instead of driving
  > Excellent! Walking doesn't use any fossil fuels at all. Every car trip you skip means less carbon dioxide in the atmosphere!

- [ ] Using more plastic bags
  > Nice try! Plastic bags are made from oil and create pollution. Using reusable bags is much better for the planet!

- [ ] Taking long hot showers
  > Nice try! Long hot showers use lots of energy to heat the water. Shorter showers save energy and reduce emissions!

## Question 2

What does 'reduce, reuse, recycle' mean?

- [ ] Buy more stuff, throw it away, buy it again
  > Nice try! That's actually the opposite! We should buy less, use things longer, and recycle when we're done.

- [x] Use less, use things again, and turn old things into new ones
  > Perfect! Reduce means use less. Reuse means use things again instead of throwing them away. Recycle means turn old materials into new products!

- [ ] A dance move
  > Nice try! It's not a dance - it's three important ways to help the environment by using less and wasting less.

- [ ] A type of exercise
  > Nice try! It's not exercise - it's a guide for using less, using things again, and recycling to help our planet.

## Question 3

Why does planting trees help fight climate change?

- [ ] Trees provide shade for picnics
  > Nice try! While shade is nice, the main climate benefit is that trees absorb carbon dioxide from the atmosphere!

- [x] Trees absorb carbon dioxide from the air
  > You've got it! Trees are nature's air cleaners. They take in carbon dioxide and store it in their wood, helping reduce greenhouse gases.

- [ ] Trees make good climbing spots
  > Nice try! Climbing trees is fun, but the climate benefit is that they absorb and store carbon dioxide!

- [ ] Trees don't actually help with climate change
  > Nice try! Trees are huge climate helpers! They absorb carbon dioxide and release oxygen. Planting trees really does make a difference.

---

## Question 4

How does turning off lights help the environment?

- [ ] It doesn't help at all
  > Nice try! Turning off lights definitely helps! Less electricity used means less fuel burned at power plants.

- [x] It saves electricity, which often comes from burning fossil fuels
  > Exactly right! Most electricity comes from power plants that burn fossil fuels. Using less electricity means fewer emissions!

- [ ] It makes rooms colder
  > Nice try! While some lights do produce heat, the main benefit of turning them off is saving electricity and reducing emissions.

- [ ] It helps you see better
  > Nice try! You actually see less in the dark! Turning off lights helps by saving energy that would otherwise come from burning fossil fuels.

## Question 5

Why is biking or walking better than driving for short trips?

- [ ] Cars are faster
  > Nice try! Cars might be faster, but for short trips, biking and walking don't use any fossil fuels at all!

- [x] Biking and walking don't use gasoline or create pollution
  > Brilliant! When you bike or walk, you're using your own energy, not gasoline. Zero emissions mean zero contribution to climate change for that trip!

- [ ] Cars are more comfortable
  > Nice try! Comfort aside, biking and walking are better for the planet because they don't burn fossil fuels.

- [ ] There's no difference
  > Nice try! There's a big difference! Cars burn gasoline and release CO2. Biking and walking produce zero emissions!

## Question 6

How can eating less meat help the environment?

- [ ] Meat is too expensive
  > Nice try! The environmental reason is that raising animals, especially cows, produces a lot of greenhouse gases.

- [x] Raising animals produces greenhouse gases like methane
  > That's right! Cows produce methane when they digest food, and raising animals uses lots of land and water. Eating more plants is gentler on our planet.

- [ ] Vegetables taste better
  > Nice try! Taste is personal, but the climate reason is that raising animals creates more greenhouse gases than growing plants.

- [ ] It doesn't help at all
  > Nice try! Reducing meat actually helps a lot! Animal farming is responsible for a large portion of greenhouse gas emissions.

## Question 7

What happens when we recycle paper instead of throwing it away?

- [ ] Nothing - it's the same
  > Nice try! Recycling paper means fewer trees need to be cut down to make new paper, and it uses less energy too!

- [x] Fewer trees need to be cut down to make new paper
  > Perfect! Recycled paper gets turned into new paper products. This saves trees that can keep absorbing carbon dioxide!

- [ ] The paper disappears
  > Nice try! Recycled paper doesn't disappear - it gets made into new paper products, saving trees and energy.

- [ ] It creates more pollution
  > Nice try! Recycling actually creates less pollution than making new paper from trees. It saves energy and resources!

## Question 8

Can kids really make a difference in fighting climate change?

- [ ] No, only adults can help
  > Nice try! Kids can absolutely make a difference! Young people around the world are leading climate action and inspiring adults to change.

- [x] Yes, every small action adds up to big change
  > Absolutely! Kids can reduce, reuse, recycle, save energy, plant trees, and inspire their families and friends. Young climate activists are changing the world!

- [ ] Only if they're very rich
  > Nice try! Anyone can help, regardless of money. Simple actions like turning off lights, recycling, and planting plants all make a difference!

- [ ] Climate change isn't real, so it doesn't matter
  > Nice try! Climate change is real and happening now. Scientists around the world agree, and kids can be part of the solution!

## Question 9

What's one way to use less plastic?

- [x] Use reusable water bottles and bags
  > Excellent! Reusable items replace hundreds of single-use plastics. One reusable water bottle can replace thousands of plastic bottles over its lifetime!

- [ ] Buy more packaged snacks
  > Nice try! Packaged snacks often come in lots of plastic. Choosing items with less packaging helps reduce plastic waste.

- [ ] Get a new plastic straw every day
  > Nice try! Using fewer straws or switching to reusable ones helps reduce plastic pollution in our oceans and landfills.

- [ ] Plastic doesn't hurt the environment
  > Nice try! Plastic pollution is a big problem. It fills landfills, pollutes oceans, and hurts wildlife. Using less plastic really helps!

## Question 10

Why is it important to talk to others about climate change?

- [ ] It's not important - keep it to yourself
  > Nice try! Sharing what you know helps more people understand and take action. Together, we can make bigger changes!

- [x] More people taking action means bigger positive impact
  > Exactly! When you share what you know, you can inspire friends and family to help too. The more people act, the bigger the difference we make together!

- [ ] It gives you something to talk about
  > Nice try! While it is interesting to discuss, the real reason is that spreading awareness leads to more people taking action!

- [ ] Only scientists should talk about it
  > Nice try! Everyone can and should talk about climate change! Young people are some of the most powerful voices for change.
