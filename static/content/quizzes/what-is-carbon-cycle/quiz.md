## Question 1

Which form of carbon is found mainly in Earth's atmosphere?

- [ ] Methane
  > Nice try! Methane is in the air, but only in a very tiny amount, so it's not the main form of carbon we find in the atmosphere.

- [ ] Bicarbonate
  > Nice try! Bicarbonate is usually found in water, like oceans and inside living things, not in the air we breathe.

- [ ] Carbonic acid
  > Nice try! Carbonic acid only forms when carbon mixes with water, so it can't mainly stay in the atmosphere.

- [x] Carbon dioxide
  > You got it right! Carbon dioxide is the main form of carbon gas present in Earth's atmosphere.

---

## Question 2

Why is carbon dioxide described as a greenhouse gas?

- [ ] It helps plants grow taller.
  > Nice try! Plants use carbon-containing gases for growth, but this is not the reason it is called a greenhouse gas.

- [x] It traps heat in the atmosphere.
  > Well done! Like a greenhouse, this gas holds heat in the air, helping warm the Earth.

- [ ] It provides oxygen for animals.
  > Nice try! Animals need oxygen to breathe, but this gas does not supply oxygen to living things.

- [ ] It breaks down food in animals.
  > Nice try! Food breakdown happens inside the body through digestion, not because of gases in the atmosphere.

---

## Question 3

A tree in the school yard is exposed to sunlight and carbon dioxide. What process is most likely happening inside its leaves?

- [ ] Respiration
  > Nice try! Respiration happens in living things, but this process releases energy—it does not use sunlight to make food.

- [ ] Combustion
  > Nice try! Combustion involves burning and heat, which does not occur inside plant leaves.

- [x] Photosynthesis
  > That's correct! Leaves use sunlight and carbon dioxide to make food for the plant.

- [ ] Decomposition
  > Nice try! Decomposition happens when organisms break down dead matter, not in a living tree.

## Question 4

Which sequence best shows how carbon moves through the food web?

- [ ] Soil, Sun, Animal, Air
  > Nice try! Carbon can be found in the soil, but it does not move from the soil to the Sun. The Sun gives energy, not carbon.

- [ ] Air, Animal, Plant, Soil
  > Nice try! Animals do not get carbon directly from the air.

- [ ] Animal, Sun, Plant, Air
  > Nice try! Carbon does not travel to or from the Sun. It moves only among living things and the environment.

- [x] Plant, Animal, Other animals
  > Well done! Carbon moves as organisms eat one another, passing carbon through the food web.

---

## Question 5

Which statement best explains why carbon connects plants, animals, and humans?

- [ ] Carbon is mainly used by plants to produce oxygen.
  > Nice try! Plants do release oxygen, but carbon is mainly used to make food for the plant.

- [x] Carbon moves through living things as food and energy.
  > Very Good! Carbon passes from plants to animals and humans through food, connecting all living organisms.

- [ ] Carbon cycles between the air and soil in natural environments.
  > Nice try! Carbon does move between air and soil, but this does not fully explain how living things are connected.

- [ ] Carbon remains in the atmosphere as a greenhouse gas that helps regulate heat.
  > Nice try! Carbon can stay in the air, but this does not show how plants, animals, and humans are linked.
