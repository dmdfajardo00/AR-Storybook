## Question 1

What can happen to species when temperature and carbon levels change?

- [x] Some species fail to survive.
  > You got it right! Some species cannot survive when temperature and carbon levels change rapidly, because their habitats or food sources may no longer support them.

- [ ] They all migrate to new areas.
  > Nice try! While some species may migrate, not all can move fast enough or find suitable habitats. Migration is not guaranteed for survival.

- [ ] They adjust instantly to new conditions.
  > Nice try! Species cannot instantly adapt to sudden environmental changes. Adaptation is a slow process over generations.

- [ ] Entire ecosystems are permanently destroyed overnight.
  > Nice try! Ecosystem shifts occur over time, not instantly. Overnight destruction is an exaggeration.

## Question 2

Why do warm-tolerant and invasive species spread to new areas with climate change?

- [ ] They avoid predators.
  > Nice try! Avoiding predators may influence movement but is not the main reason warm-tolerant and invasive species spread.

- [ ] They follow human settlements.
  > Nice try! Some species are affected by humans, but this is not the main driver of climate-driven range expansion.

- [x] The conditions become favorable.
  > You got it right! Climate change creates new conditions that allow warm-tolerant and invasive species to survive and spread in new areas.

- [ ] They move randomly without any environmental reason.
  > Nice try! Species movement is guided by environmental factors, not random chance.

## Question 3

Which marine organisms are highly sensitive to ocean warming and acidification?

- [ ] Deep-sea predators
  > Nice try! Deep-sea predators are generally less sensitive to changes in ocean temperature and acidity than some other organisms.

- [x] Corals and planktons
  > You got it right! Corals and plankton are highly sensitive to temperature and acidity changes in oceans, affecting their survival and reproduction.

- [ ] Fast-swimming fishes
  > Nice try! Some fish are affected by temperature changes, but they are generally less sensitive.

- [ ] Large whales and sharks
  > Nice try! Large marine animals are affected indirectly by environmental changes and are not the most sensitive organisms to ocean acidification.

## Question 4

How does melting glaciers affect polar ecosystems?

- [ ] Makes ice safer to walk on
  > Nice try! Melting glaciers reduce, not improve, safety or habitat availability.

- [ ] Increases fish populations in the area
  > Nice try! Melting glaciers disrupt habitats and do not automatically increase fish populations.

- [x] Reduces natural habitats for polar species
  > You got it right! Melting glaciers and ice caps reduce habitats for species such as polar bears and penguins.

- [ ] Causes polar ecosystems to become deserts overnight
  > Nice try! Ecosystems do not transform overnight. This is an exaggerated and unrealistic scenario.

## Question 5

A skeptic argues that animals can simply migrate to new areas to avoid the effects of climate change. Why is this argument flawed?

- [ ] All animals can live anywhere if they try.
  > Nice try! Species have specific habitat requirements and cannot survive anywhere.

- [ ] Migration prevents climate change from happening.
  > Nice try! Migration does not stop climate change; it only affects individual species' survival.

- [ ] All species can live anywhere if conditions are extreme.
  > Nice try! Extreme conditions limit survival. Species cannot tolerate all climates.

- [x] Many species cannot move quickly enough to reach new habitats.
  > You got it right! Many species cannot migrate fast enough or find suitable habitats, so migration alone is not a reliable solution.
