## Question 1

Which human activity reduces the number of trees that remove carbon from the atmosphere?

- [x] Deforestation
  > You got it right! Deforestation removes large areas of trees, reducing the amount of carbon dioxide absorbed from the atmosphere.

- [ ] Mining operations
  > Nice try! Mining can disturb soil and release some carbon, but it does not directly remove large numbers of trees from the ecosystem.

- [ ] Burning fossil fuels in factories
  > Nice try! Fossil fuel burning releases carbon dioxide into the atmosphere, but it does not directly reduce the number of trees.

- [ ] Building cities and clearing forests
  > Nice try! While urban development can remove trees, the main term for large-scale removal of forests is deforestation.

## Question 2

Why does burning fossil fuels increase carbon dioxide in the atmosphere?

- [ ] It creates oxygen for plants.
  > Nice try! Burning fossil fuels does not produce oxygen; it releases carbon dioxide instead.

- [x] It releases carbon stored underground.
  > You got it right! Fossil fuels store carbon that was trapped millions of years ago, and burning them releases this carbon as CO2.

- [ ] It reduces the carbon in oceans temporarily.
  > Nice try! Ocean carbon levels are affected indirectly, but burning fossil fuels primarily adds CO2 to the atmosphere.

- [ ] It warms the air and melts ice faster than normal.
  > Nice try! This is a consequence of greenhouse gas accumulation, not the direct reason CO2 increases.

## Question 3

A mining site exposes soil and rocks that store carbon. Which effect does this have on the carbon cycle?

- [ ] It prevents photosynthesis in nearby plants.
  > Nice try! Mining may disturb land, but the main effect on the carbon cycle is releasing stored carbon.

- [ ] It removes oxygen from the soil temporarily.
  > Nice try! Oxygen levels in soil may change, but this is not the primary effect on carbon movement.

- [x] It releases stored carbon and greenhouse gases.
  > You got it right! Mining exposes soil and rocks, releasing stored carbon as CO2 or methane and affecting the carbon cycle.

- [ ] It increases carbon absorption by animals and plants.
  > Nice try! Mining does not enhance carbon absorption; it generally releases carbon back into the atmosphere.

## Question 4

Which combination of human activities contributes most to increasing atmospheric CO2?

- [ ] Mining and urban water use
  > Nice try! Mining contributes some CO2, but water use has little effect on atmospheric carbon levels.

- [ ] Using solar panels and planting trees
  > Nice try! These activities actually reduce CO2 in the atmosphere, not increase it.

- [x] Deforestation and fossil fuel burning
  > You got it right! Removing forests reduces CO2 absorption and burning fossil fuels releases large amounts of CO2, making these the main contributors.

- [ ] Agriculture and wind energy generation
  > Nice try! Agriculture can release some carbon through soil disturbance, but wind energy does not produce CO2.

## Question 5

Which statement best explains why human activities disrupt the carbon cycle?

- [ ] They create more oxygen than plants can use.
  > Nice try! Human activities do not create oxygen; the disruption comes from altering carbon flow.

- [ ] They reduce sunlight reaching forests and oceans.
  > Nice try! Sunlight changes do not directly disrupt the carbon cycle; the main issue is how humans move carbon faster than nature can recycle it.

- [ ] They store carbon underground safely for millions of years.
  > Nice try! Storing carbon underground can actually reduce atmospheric CO2; the disruption comes from releasing carbon too quickly.

- [x] They remove carbon from one area faster than nature can recycle it.
  > You got it right! Human activities like deforestation, fossil fuel burning, and mining release or remove carbon faster than natural processes can handle, disrupting the carbon cycle.
