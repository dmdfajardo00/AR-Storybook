## Question 1

Which agricultural practice releases carbon from soil?

- [x] Tillage
  > You got it right! Tillage disturbs the soil and exposes stored carbon to oxygen, causing carbon dioxide to be released into the atmosphere.

- [ ] Irrigation
  > Nice try! Irrigation adds water to soil but does not directly release stored carbon into the air.

- [ ] Crop rotation
  > Nice try! Crop rotation helps maintain soil health and can actually improve carbon storage rather than release it.

- [ ] Planting cover crops
  > Nice try! Cover crops protect soil and help keep carbon stored, reducing carbon loss instead of increasing it.

## Question 2

Why do livestock contribute to greenhouse gas emissions?

- [ ] They eat too many plants.
  > Nice try! Eating plants does not directly produce greenhouse gases. The emissions come from biological processes inside the animals.

- [ ] They reduce soil carbon by grazing.
  > Nice try! Grazing can affect soil carbon, but it is not the main reason livestock increase greenhouse gas emissions.

- [x] They produce methane during digestion.
  > You got it right! Livestock release methane during digestion, a powerful greenhouse gas that adds carbon to the atmosphere.

- [ ] They convert freshwater into carbon-rich gases.
  > Nice try! Livestock do not convert water into gases. Greenhouse gases come from digestion and waste processes.

## Question 3

How do land use changes like converting forests into cities affect the carbon cycle?

- [x] Fewer plants absorb less CO2.
  > You got it right! Removing forests reduces the number of plants that absorb carbon dioxide, leaving more carbon in the atmosphere.

- [ ] Carbon is stored in urban structures.
  > Nice try! Buildings may contain materials with carbon, but they do not function as active carbon sinks like plants.

- [ ] Soil stores more carbon under buildings.
  > Nice try! Covered soil usually stores less carbon because plant growth and soil processes are reduced.

- [ ] Cities create new forests that absorb more carbon.
  > Nice try! Urbanization usually removes forests rather than creating new ones that absorb carbon.

## Question 4

Which human activity releases both methane and carbon dioxide from waste?

- [ ] Recycling materials
  > Nice try! Recycling reduces waste and greenhouse gas emissions instead of releasing methane and carbon dioxide.

- [x] Landfill decomposition
  > You got it right! As waste decomposes in landfills, it releases methane and carbon dioxide into the atmosphere.

- [ ] Composting food waste
  > Nice try! Composting mainly produces carbon dioxide and is managed to reduce methane emissions.

- [ ] Storing waste in sealed containers
  > Nice try! Sealed storage limits gas release rather than increasing greenhouse gas emissions.

## Question 5

Which statement best explains the effect of human activities on the carbon cycle?

- [ ] Carbon naturally disappears without human actions.
  > Nice try! Carbon is conserved and cycles through Earth systems; it does not disappear on its own.

- [x] Farming and waste management release carbon into the air.
  > You got it right! Many human activities release stored carbon faster than natural systems can recycle it.

- [ ] Human settlements increase carbon absorption of the ecosystems.
  > Nice try! Human settlements usually reduce vegetation and carbon absorption.

- [ ] All human activities remove carbon completely from the environment.
  > Nice try! Carbon cannot be removed completely. It only changes form and location.
