## Question 1

Which process allows forests to remove carbon dioxide from the atmosphere?

- [ ] Respiration
  > Nice try! Respiration releases carbon dioxide rather than removing it from the air.

- [ ] Combustion
  > Nice try! Combustion produces carbon dioxide when fuels burn, instead of removing it.

- [x] Photosynthesis
  > You got it right! Photosynthesis is how trees absorb carbon dioxide from the air and release oxygen.

- [ ] Decomposition
  > Nice try! Decomposition releases carbon stored in organic matter, rather than removing carbon dioxide from the atmosphere.

## Question 2

Why are forests important in regulating Earth's temperature?

- [x] They store carbon.
  > You got it right! Forests help store carbon, reduce greenhouse gases and help regulate climate.

- [ ] They block sunlight.
  > Nice try! Forests do provide shade, but that doesn't directly regulate global temperature.

- [ ] They reduce rainfall.
  > Nice try! Forests can influence local rainfall patterns, but this doesn't explain temperature regulation.

- [ ] They increase oxygen production in cities.
  > Nice try! Oxygen production supports life but does not directly cool the planet.

## Question 3

Which action does not support reducing carbon emissions in daily life?

- [ ] Walking
  > Nice try! Walking is a low-carbon transportation method.

- [ ] Riding a bicycle
  > Nice try! Cycling avoids carbon emissions from fossil fuels.

- [ ] Using an electric vehicle
  > Nice try! Electric vehicles reduce emissions.

- [x] Driving a gasoline-powered car alone
  > You got it right! Driving alone in a gasoline car releases significant carbon into the atmosphere.

## Question 4

A community replaces most car travel with biking and walking. What is the most direct environmental effect?

- [ ] Less traffic
  > Nice try! Traffic reduction is a social benefit, but the immediate environmental effect is on emissions.

- [ ] Lower fuel use
  > Nice try! Reduced fuel consumption occurs, but it is a secondary effect rather than the direct impact on carbon.

- [x] Reduced carbon emissions
  > You got it right! Replacing car travel with biking and walking directly reduces carbon emissions.

- [ ] Improved ecosystem health
  > Nice try! Ecosystem health benefits over time but is not the most direct effect.

## Question 5

Which statement best explains how small human actions affect the environment?

- [ ] They are insignificant.
  > Nice try! Individual actions may seem small but can accumulate to large effects.

- [ ] They rarely create impact.
  > Nice try! Even small actions contribute when performed collectively.

- [ ] They only matter in large numbers.
  > Nice try! Some individual actions have measurable effects even before aggregation.

- [x] They collectively influence ecosystem stability.
  > You got it right! Combined small actions can meaningfully protect ecosystems and help balance the carbon cycle.
