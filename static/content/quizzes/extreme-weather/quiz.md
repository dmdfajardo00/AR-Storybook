## Question 1

How does climate change affect weather?

- [ ] It makes weather more mild and predictable
  > Nice try! Climate change actually does the opposite - it makes weather more extreme and harder to predict.

- [x] It makes weather more extreme and unpredictable
  > Correct! As Earth warms, we see stronger storms, longer droughts, more intense heat waves, and heavier rainfall - weather becomes more extreme.

- [ ] It has no effect on weather
  > Nice try! Climate change has a big effect on weather. Scientists have observed more extreme weather events as temperatures rise.

- [ ] It only affects weather at the poles
  > Nice try! Climate change affects weather everywhere on Earth, not just at the poles. Extreme weather events are happening worldwide.

## Question 2

What is a drought?

- [x] A very long period without enough rain
  > Exactly! Droughts happen when an area doesn't get enough rain for a long time. This can hurt crops, wildlife, and people's water supply.

- [ ] A type of strong wind
  > Nice try! Strong winds are different from droughts. A drought is when there isn't enough rain for a long time.

- [ ] A very cold winter
  > Nice try! Cold winters are different from droughts. A drought is a long period without enough rainfall.

- [ ] A sudden flood
  > Nice try! Floods are actually the opposite of droughts! A drought is when there's not enough water.

> Hint: Think about what happens when it doesn't rain for a very long time.

## Question 3

Why are hurricanes getting stronger due to climate change?

- [ ] Because there's more wind in the atmosphere
  > Nice try! It's actually about water temperature, not wind. Hurricanes get their energy from warm ocean water.

- [x] Because warmer ocean waters give hurricanes more energy
  > Brilliant! Hurricanes are like heat engines - they get their power from warm water. As oceans warm up, hurricanes can become stronger.

- [ ] Because there are more clouds
  > Nice try! Clouds alone don't make hurricanes stronger. Warm ocean water is what gives hurricanes their energy.

- [ ] Hurricanes aren't getting stronger
  > Nice try! Scientists have observed that hurricanes are becoming more intense. Warmer oceans give them more energy to grow.

> Hint: Hurricanes form over oceans. What's happening to ocean temperatures?

## Question 4

What problems can extreme heat waves cause?

- [x] They can make people sick and damage crops
  > That's right! Extreme heat is dangerous for people, especially older adults and children. It can also dry out and kill crops.

- [ ] They make plants grow faster
  > Nice try! While plants need warmth, extreme heat actually hurts them by drying them out and damaging their cells.

- [ ] They don't cause any problems
  > Nice try! Extreme heat waves are very dangerous. They can cause illness in people, kill crops, and start wildfires.

- [ ] They only affect deserts
  > Nice try! Heat waves can happen anywhere and affect cities, farms, forests, and all kinds of places.

## Question 5

How does climate change affect rainfall?

- [ ] It makes rainfall the same everywhere
  > Nice try! Climate change actually makes rainfall more uneven - some places get much more rain while others get much less.

- [x] Some places get more rain and flooding, while others get less rain and drought
  > Excellent! Climate change changes weather patterns. Some areas experience heavier rainfall and floods, while others face more droughts.

- [ ] It stops all rain completely
  > Nice try! Rain doesn't stop completely, but the patterns change. Some places get too much rain, others get too little.

- [ ] Climate change only affects temperature, not rain
  > Nice try! Climate change affects all parts of weather, including how much rain falls and where it falls.

## Question 6

What can communities do to prepare for extreme weather?

- [x] Build better warning systems and emergency plans
  > Great thinking! Communities can prepare by having warning systems, emergency shelters, and plans to help people stay safe during extreme weather.

- [ ] Nothing - there's no way to prepare
  > Nice try! There's actually a lot we can do! Building better shelters, creating warning systems, and having emergency plans all help.

- [ ] Move everyone to the moon
  > Nice try! We can't move to the moon, but we can prepare right here on Earth with good planning and preparation.

- [ ] Just hope extreme weather doesn't happen
  > Nice try! Hoping isn't a plan! Communities need real preparations like warning systems, shelters, and emergency supplies.

## Question 7

What is a heat wave?

- [ ] A wave in the ocean that's warm
  > Nice try! A heat wave isn't in the ocean - it's when the air stays extremely hot for several days in a row.

- [x] A period of unusually hot weather lasting several days
  > Perfect! A heat wave is when temperatures are much higher than normal for several days. This can be dangerous for people, animals, and plants.

- [ ] A type of sunburn
  > Nice try! Sunburn happens to skin in the sun. A heat wave is unusually hot weather that lasts for days.

- [ ] A warm breeze
  > Nice try! A warm breeze is gentle, but a heat wave is when it's extremely hot for many days in a row.

## Question 8

Why are wildfires becoming more common?

- [ ] Because people are starting more fires
  > Nice try! While some fires are human-caused, the main reason wildfires are worse is that hotter, drier conditions make forests more likely to burn.

- [x] Because hotter, drier conditions make forests easier to burn
  > Exactly! Climate change creates hotter temperatures and longer droughts. This dries out trees and plants, making them much easier to catch fire.

- [ ] Because there are more trees now
  > Nice try! Actually, we're losing forests. The problem is that hot, dry weather makes the trees we have more likely to burn.

- [ ] Wildfires aren't becoming more common
  > Nice try! Scientists have recorded that wildfires are happening more often and burning larger areas than before.

## Question 9

How does flooding affect communities?

- [ ] It only gets basements wet
  > Nice try! Floods can do much more damage than that - they can destroy homes, wash out roads, and contaminate drinking water.

- [x] It can destroy homes, roads, and make people lose their belongings
  > That's right! Flooding is very destructive. It can damage buildings, wash away roads and bridges, and force people to leave their homes.

- [ ] It helps plants grow better
  > Nice try! While some water is good for plants, floods are too much water at once and can destroy crops and gardens.

- [ ] Flooding doesn't cause any problems
  > Nice try! Flooding is one of the most damaging types of extreme weather, causing billions of dollars in damage every year.

## Question 10

What's the connection between climate change and food supplies?

- [ ] Climate change makes all food tastier
  > Nice try! Climate change doesn't affect taste, but it does make it harder to grow enough food because of droughts, floods, and extreme heat.

- [x] Extreme weather can damage crops and make food harder to grow
  > Correct! Droughts dry out crops, floods wash them away, and extreme heat can kill plants. This makes food more expensive and harder to find.

- [ ] Climate change has nothing to do with food
  > Nice try! Climate change has a big effect on food. Farmers depend on predictable weather, and extreme events can destroy their crops.

- [ ] There will always be plenty of food no matter what
  > Nice try! Unfortunately, climate change threatens our food supply. We need to protect our planet to make sure everyone has enough to eat.
