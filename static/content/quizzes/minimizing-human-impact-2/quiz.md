## Question 1

Which energy source produces little to no carbon emissions during use?

- [x] Solar
  > You got it right! Solar panels generate electricity without releasing carbon dioxide, supporting a cleaner atmosphere.

- [ ] Natural gas
  > Nice try! Burning natural gas produces carbon dioxide, so it contributes to atmospheric carbon.

- [ ] Coal power
  > Nice try! Coal combustion is a major source of carbon emissions.

- [ ] Diesel-based electricity
  > Nice try! Diesel engines emit carbon and other pollutants during electricity generation.

## Question 2

Why is renewable energy considered sustainable?

- [ ] It is cheap.
  > Nice try! Cost can vary, but sustainability is about resource use and emissions.

- [ ] It is unlimited.
  > Nice try! While some resources are abundant, this alone does not define sustainability.

- [ ] It replaces nuclear power.
  > Nice try! Renewable energy does not specifically target nuclear energy.

- [x] It relies on resources that do not run out and emit less carbon.
  > You got it right! Renewable energy reduces reliance on fossil fuels and produces minimal carbon emissions.

## Question 3

Which action specifically reduces methane emissions from waste?

- [ ] Recycling
  > Nice try! Recycling reduces energy use but does not prevent methane from decomposing food waste.

- [x] Composting
  > You got it right! Composting organic matter prevents methane formation that occurs when food decomposes anaerobically in landfills.

- [ ] Using plastic bags
  > Nice try! Plastic does not address methane release from organic waste.

- [ ] Throwing food scraps in landfills
  > Nice try! Landfilling organic waste produces methane, a potent greenhouse gas.

---

## Question 4

Why does composting food waste reduce greenhouse gas emissions?

- [ ] It helps manage waste volume more efficiently.
  > Nice try! Reducing waste volume is a benefit, but it does not explain the greenhouse gas reduction.

- [ ] It reduces unpleasant odors during waste storage.
  > Nice try! Odor reduction is a minor effect and not the environmental mechanism.

- [x] It allows organic material to decompose in the presence of oxygen.
  > You got it right! Aerobic decomposition during composting prevents methane, a powerful greenhouse gas, from forming.

- [ ] It prevents the formation of carbon dioxide and bicarbonate during decay.
  > Nice try! Carbon dioxide is naturally produced in aerobic decomposition, and bicarbonate is not a major gas emitted in landfills.

## Question 5

What is the primary environmental benefit of switching from gasoline-powered cars to electric vehicles?

- [ ] It makes travel quieter in cities.
  > Nice try! Reduced noise is a benefit but not the primary environmental impact.

- [ ] It lowers the cost of daily commuting over time.
  > Nice try! Cost savings are economic, not environmental.

- [ ] It reduces direct emissions of carbon dioxide into the air.
  > Nice try! Reducing emissions is important, but EVs also decrease reliance on fossil fuels, affecting the carbon cycle.

- [x] It decreases reliance on fossil fuels while helping balance the carbon cycle.
  > You got it right! EVs reduce fossil fuel use and support the carbon balance in the environment.
