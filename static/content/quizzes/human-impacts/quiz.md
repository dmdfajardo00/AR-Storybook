## Question 1

What happens when excess carbon dioxide builds up in the atmosphere?

- [x] Heat is trapped.
  > You got it right! Carbon dioxide is a greenhouse gas that traps heat in the atmosphere, leading to climate warming.

- [ ] Clouds form faster.
  > Nice try! Cloud formation depends on water vapor and air movement, not directly on carbon dioxide buildup.

- [ ] Wind speed increases globally.
  > Nice try! Wind patterns may change with climate, but increased wind speed is not a direct result of excess carbon dioxide.

- [ ] Earth's magnetic field becomes weaker.
  > Nice try! Earth's magnetic field is related to movements in the planet's core, not greenhouse gases.

## Question 2

Which human-caused factor is the primary driver of climate warming?

- [x] Increased CO2 levels
  > You got it right! Rising carbon dioxide from human activities traps more heat and is the main driver of climate warming.

- [ ] Shifts in seasonal rainfall distribution
  > Nice try! Changes in rainfall are effects of climate warming, not the main cause.

- [ ] Changes in ocean circulation patterns
  > Nice try! Ocean circulation can influence climate, but it is not the primary human-caused driver of warming.

- [ ] Loss of polar ice over long time periods
  > Nice try! Melting ice is a result of warming, not the factor that causes it.

## Question 3

Why does the ocean become more acidic as it absorbs carbon dioxide?

- [ ] Salt levels change.
  > Nice try! Salt levels affect salinity, but they do not directly cause ocean acidity.

- [ ] Oxygen dissolves differently.
  > Nice try! Oxygen levels may change, but they do not explain why acidity increases.

- [ ] Water circulation patterns shift as ocean currents move heat.
  > Nice try! Ocean currents move heat, but they do not directly cause acidification.

- [x] Carbon dioxide reacts with seawater to form acids that lower ocean pH.
  > You got it right! When CO2 dissolves in seawater, it forms acids that reduce pH, making the ocean more acidic.

## Question 4

Which marine organisms are most affected by ocean acidification?

- [ ] Deep-sea predators
  > Nice try! Many deep-sea predators are less directly affected by changes in ocean acidity.

- [ ] Fast-swimming fishes
  > Nice try! Most fast-swimming fish can regulate their internal body chemistry, making them less sensitive to changes in ocean acidity.

- [x] Shell-forming organisms
  > You got it right! Acidic water makes it harder for organisms like corals and shellfish to build and maintain shells.

- [ ] Marine animals that live in warm water
  > Nice try! Living in warm water affects temperature tolerance, but it does not determine how organisms respond to changes in ocean acidity.

## Question 5

How are climate warming and ocean acidification connected to the carbon cycle?

- [ ] Both occur only in oceans.
  > Nice try! Climate warming mainly affects the atmosphere, while ocean acidification affects seawater.

- [x] Both result from excess carbon.
  > You got it right! Extra carbon from human activities drives both warming in the air and acidification in the oceans.

- [ ] Both depend on natural disasters.
  > Nice try! Natural disasters may be affected by climate change, but they are not the main cause of these processes.

- [ ] Both are caused by sunlight alone.
  > Nice try! Sunlight provides energy, but excess carbon is the key factor behind these changes.
