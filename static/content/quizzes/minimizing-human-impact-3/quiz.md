## Question 1

Which farming practice increases carbon stored in soil?

- [x] No-till
  > You got it right! No-till farming preserves soil carbon by minimizing disturbance and erosion.

- [ ] Crop rotation
  > Nice try! Crop rotation improves soil fertility but does not directly maximize carbon storage.

- [ ] Cover cropping
  > Nice try! Cover crops add organic matter but alone do not retain as much carbon as no-till.

- [ ] Repeated deep plowing
  > Nice try! Deep plowing releases carbon stored in the soil instead of preserving it.

---

## Question 2

Why is healthy soil important in the carbon cycle?

- [ ] It prevents erosion from rainfall.
  > Nice try! Preventing erosion protects land but does not explain carbon storage.

- [ ] It stores water for irrigation purposes.
  > Nice try! Water retention is beneficial for plants, but not the key factor for carbon balance.

- [x] It acts as a long-term carbon reservoir.
  > You got it right! Healthy soil stores carbon, helping regulate greenhouse gases and support the carbon cycle.

- [ ] It supports plant growth and nutrient cycling.
  > Nice try! Plant growth contributes indirectly, but carbon storage is the primary role.

## Question 3

Which action best represents sustainable agriculture?

- [ ] Monocropping the same crop each year.
  > Nice try! Monocropping reduces soil health and carbon retention.

- [ ] Using heavy pesticides to kill pests quickly.
  > Nice try! Pesticides harm ecosystems and do not improve carbon storage.

- [x] Rotating crops to maintain soil organic matter.
  > You got it right! Crop rotation maintains soil fertility and increases carbon-rich organic matter.

- [ ] Leaving fields bare in between planting seasons.
  > Nice try! Bare soil loses carbon and nutrients, reducing sustainability.

## Question 4

How does environmental education help reduce environmental problems?

- [ ] It provides additional funding for school programs.
  > Nice try! Funding helps schools, but it does not directly change environmental behavior.

- [ ] It spreads rules about what students can and cannot do.
  > Nice try! Rules alone do not teach the reasoning needed to protect the environment.

- [ ] It encourages students to participate in unrelated competitions.
  > Nice try! Competitions not linked to environmental learning do not help reduce problems.

- [x] It equips learners with knowledge to make responsible decisions.
  > You got it right! Education prepares students to take informed actions that positively affect the environment.

## Question 5

Which statement best summarizes the role of education and agriculture in environmental protection?

- [ ] They help indirectly without major impact.
  > Nice try! Education and sustainable agriculture have direct and meaningful impacts.

- [ ] They are optional for communities to follow.
  > Nice try! Participation is important for maintaining ecosystems and carbon balance.

- [ ] They only support technological advances in farming.
  > Nice try! Technology is part of the solution, but knowledge and practice also play key roles.

- [x] They promote informed actions that sustain ecosystems and carbon balance.
  > You got it right! Combining education with sustainable practices allows humans to protect ecosystems effectively.
